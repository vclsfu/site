About Us

Vietnamese Canadian League (VCL) is founded by Vietnamese-Canadian university students at Simon Fraser University (SFU) to empower fellow peers to promote unity and to bridge the gap between Vietnamese and Vietnamese Canadian traditions through leadership and community. We are dedicated to educating the whole person and empowering student leaders for the future in a just and humane world.

Our Mission

We are a cultural, nonprofit organization dedicated to the general development of the student body. Like other chapters worldwide, our purpose is to provide an environment for students with Vietnamese heritage and/or interest in Vietnam to maintain and promote Vietnamese culture and tradition, have fun, assist each other, make life-long friends, and build leadership skills throughout their university careers.

Our Approach

We want to establish ourselves as a different entity from the current VSA on campus. The VCL is driven by our fundamental principles that can be summed up in three pillars. Though we are an organization that focuses on the Vietnamese Canadian experience, we are honored to be able to count many members of non-Vietnamese heritage as part of our family. We welcome any and all students to come see what VCL has to offer!

Our Pillars

  • Culture:   We recognize that we have a unique Vietnamese heritage and tradition and we aim to preserve and to learn more about our culture.
  • Philanthropy:   We recognize the diverse contemporary issues facing our community and we attempt to understand them, to promote awareness about them, and to address them.
  • Leadership:   We recognize and embrace the opportunities that we have been given and endeavour in empowering tomorrow’s leaders through strategic initiatives and programs.

Projects

Family Feud for Funds

Central Vietnam Flood Fundraiser
Vietnamese Canada League (VCL) collaborated with over 10 Vietnamese Student Associations (VSA) across North America to raise funds for the flooding in Central Vietnam. The flood had caused over 250 deaths, over 60 missing individuals and millions of casualties due to the flood and landslides. The flood not only impacted vulnerable civilians, but it also damaged lands for agriculture and livestock farming. Through this collaborative fundraising event, VCL had raised over $1,400.00 and had over 10 teams registered to participate in our Family Feud for Flood event. To further support the families affected by the flood, VCL has donated all proceeds raised in this event to the Blue Dragon Children's Foundation and the Children of Vietnam Benevolent Foundation to help these organizations build flood-proof homes and provide emergency kits for the ones in need.

Community Safety

VCL is committed to taking all reasonable steps to prevent harassment and abuse and will make every reasonable effort promptly and completely to address and correct any misconduct that may occur. Any individual is able to submit an incident report, an internal review request, and/or a negligence report knowing our Executive Board will do our best to resolve the incident and maintain a safe community.

To make a report of any incident that occur, please contact VCL through our email at club@vclsfu.ca
